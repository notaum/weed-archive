### **Evolvulus alsinoides (Vishnukranti)**

  

**1. Taxonomic insights**

Species: _Evolvulus alsinoides_

Family: Convolvulaceae

Genus: _Evolvulus_

Related Herbs from the same family:

- _Ipomoea batatas_ (Sweet Potato) - A common food crop, the leaves of which are also used as a nutritious vegetable.
    
- _Argyreia nervosa_ (Elephant Creeper, Vridhadaru) - Used in Ayurveda as a rasayana (rejuvenator) and for nervous system disorders, but requires caution.
    

The Convolvulaceae family is primarily known for its climbing plants, many of which are ornamental or agricultural. _Evolvulus_ is a notable genus for its medicinal value.

**3. Medicinal Uses**Medhya (Brain Tonic), Nootropic (enhances memory and cognition), Anxiolytic (reduces anxiety), Antidepressant, Antipyretic (fever-reducing), Anti-inflammatory, Blood Purifier.

**4. Phytochemicals specific to the plant and their action.**

Alkaloids (Shankhapushpine, Evolvine): These are considered the primary bioactive compounds responsible for the plant's profound effects on the central nervous system, acting as a **Nootropic** and **Anxiolytic**.

Scopoletin (a Coumarin): This compound has been studied for its **Antidepressant** and sedative properties, contributing to the calming effect of the herb.

Beta-Sitosterol: A phytosterol with **Anti-inflammatory** and cholesterol-lowering properties.

Flavonoids and Tannins: Contribute to the **Antioxidant** and **Blood Purifying** (depurative) actions, helping to neutralize toxins.

**5. Traditional and Ethnobotanical uses covering the Medicinal uses.**

Medhya Rasayana (Brain Tonic for Memory and Intellect)

Formulation: Powder (churna) or juice of the whole plant.

Preparation & Use: The dried plant is powdered and mixed with milk or ghee. The fresh juice is also consumed. It is taken daily to enhance memory, focus, and cognitive function.

Reasoning: The alkaloids are believed to improve cholinergic function in the brain and protect neurons from oxidative stress, thereby improving learning and memory retention.

Anxiolytic & Antidepressant (for Stress and Mental Health)

Formulation: Powder or decoction.

Preparation & Use: The powder is taken with milk or water to calm the mind, reduce anxiety, and alleviate symptoms of depression.

Reasoning: Scopoletin and the alkaloids have a calming effect on the nervous system, promoting a sense of well-being without causing significant sedation.

Antipyretic & Blood Purifier (for Fevers and Skin Diseases)

Formulation: Juice or decoction.

Preparation & Use: The fresh juice of the plant is given to reduce fever. It is also used as a blood purifier in the treatment of skin conditions like eczema and psoriasis.

Reasoning: The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant compounds help reduce fever and eliminate toxins from the blood, which are often implicated in chronic skin diseases.

**6. Healing recipes, Medicated Oils, Teas and Culinary uses (if any).**

It is used medicinally, not as a food item.

Classic Medhya Rasayana (Brain Tonic)

Purpose: To boost memory, intelligence, and mental clarity.

Preparation & Use:

- Take 3-5 grams of the dried, powdered _Evolvulus alsinoides_ plant (whole).
    
- Mix with 1 teaspoon of cow's ghee and 1 teaspoon of honey.
    
- Consume this mixture every morning on an empty stomach for at least 1-2 months for best results.
    

Calming Tea for Anxiety

Purpose: To relieve stress and promote mental calm.

Preparation & Use:

- Take 1-2 grams of the dried powder.
    
- Steep in a cup of hot water for 5-10 minutes.
    
- Strain and drink once or twice a day.
    

**7. Disclaimer:**_Evolvulus alsinoides_ is a powerful nervine tonic. While it is generally safe, its effects on the brain are profound. It is crucial to use the correct species, as the name "Shankhapushpi" is also used for other herbs like _Convolvulus pluricaulis_ and _Canscora decussata_. Consultation with an Ayurvedic practitioner is recommended to determine the right herb and dosage for your constitution. Not recommended for self-medication during pregnancy. **9. Further study: Plants that might interest you due to similar medicinal properties**

1. **_Bacopa monnieri_** **(Brahmi):** Scientific name: _Bacopa monnieri_. Family: Plantaginaceae. Similarity: This is the other premier Medhya (brain tonic) herb in Ayurveda. Both _Bacopa_ and _Evolvulus_ are used to enhance memory, learning, and cognitive function and to reduce anxiety.
    
2. **_Centella asiatica_** **(Gotu Kola, Mandukaparni):** Scientific name: _Centella asiatica_. Family: Apiaceae. Similarity: Another renowned brain tonic and nervine, used to improve memory, heal nervous tissue, and reduce stress. It forms a trio of major cognitive enhancers in Ayurveda along with Brahmi and Shankhapushpi.
    
3. **_Convolvulus pluricaulis_** **(Shankhapushpi):** Scientific name: _Convolvulus pluricaulis_. Family: Convolvulaceae. Similarity: Belonging to the same family, it is one of the primary plants recognized as "Shankhapushpi" in different regions of India and is used identically for its nootropic and calming effects.
